Introduction

Sainik Awasiya Mahavidyalaya, Dharan was established on July 7, 2005 in Sunsari District, Dharan Sub-Metropolitan City, Ward No. 13, Fusre with the sacred objective of carrying out all round development and positive transformation of the society and the nation through education through education. The motto of this Mahavidyalaya is "ज्ञानेन परमो सेवा", meaning ‘providing knowledge is the greatest service’. The objective of this Mahavidyalaya is to prepare patriotic, disciplined, competent and dutiful citizens by imparting quality and century-appropriate education to the talented children of the in-service and retired Nepali Army personnel and the civilians within the national education system and education policy. It has been achieving excellent academic and other achievements by developing all-round development of students through different ECA/CCA activities. In the quotas fixed for each academic session, 60% military and 40% non-military students in class 6 and class 11 (Science) are admitted through open entrance examination every year. Till the end of the academic session 2077 BS, the Mahavidyalaya has a total of 837 students including 575 male and 267 female students from class 6 to 12 (science faculty), 59 teachers, 71 staff and 35 military personnel.

Extra Curriculam Activitics

SAM Dharan Facilities

TRANSPORTATION

This Mahavidyalaya has 4 school buses and a microbus for day the girl students who are day scholar. The buses reach up to Biratchowk in the east. There is a regular bus route for the girl students from Itahari and throughout Dharan City.

LIBRARY

The concept of ‘E-library’ is going to materialize from this session, though there are an ample amount of hard copies of reference materials and literature for students from its establishment in 2062. There is a trend of adding a sufficient amount of current versions of reference materials and textbooks including best-selling literature from home and abroad to make the library updated and lively. The library subscribes daily national and local newspapers, periodicals, and magazines for up-to-date news of the world. All students enrolled in this Mahavidyalaya can be the member of the central library and get the book of their interest for certain periods of time frequently.

Hostel

In this Mahavidyalaya, the male students must stay in the hostel as the residential students whereas the female students are completely day scholars. However, the girls who are preparing for the board exams of classes 10, 11, and 12 may be residential for few months as per their parents’ request. In this academic session 2078, there are 573 students in this Mahavidyalaya. All the residential students are accommodated in the four houses namely Kanchanjunga, Sagarmatha, Annapurna, and Dhaulagiri along with housemasters, assistant housemasters, and house guards who take care of them round the clock. In order to discipline the students living in the houses of this Mahavidyalaya and to assist them in their studies, housemasters of different subjects have been appointed in each house. Attempts have been made to alleviate the loneliness of the students by involving them in various programs on holidays. The various activities conducted in the hostel are mentioned in the academic calendar of the Mahavidyalaya and are conducted in a systematic manner. Competitive spirit has been developed in the students living in different houses of this Mahavidyalaya by organizing sports competitions from time to time. Such competitive events held on special days or occasions; create a fun atmosphere by removing the loneliness of the students. In order to make the students feel social responsibility, the activities including cleaning inside and outside the Mahavidyalaya during the holidays are remarkable. Students have developed the habit of making good use of time and completing their work on time when they have to follow different time schedules in winter and summer.
